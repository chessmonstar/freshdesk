# Luna Cycle — Support Policy Knowledge Base

This file is injected into every draft as ground truth. Keep it short, current, and
factual. When a policy changes (a fee, a lead time, a link), edit it HERE — the agent
will immediately use the new value. Anything not covered here should be hedged or
escalated, never invented.

## Shipping & delivery
- Bikes ship within the U.S. and Canada only. Small parts/accessories may ship elsewhere.
- Typical handling lead time: 2–3 business days before a bike ships.
- Bikes ship via FedEx Freight. Freight transit from L.A.: a few days to the West Coast,
  4–5 days to the middle of the country, ~6–10 days to the East Coast.
- FedEx Freight calls the customer to schedule a delivery appointment once the bike
  reaches their local terminal. Until then, tracking shows an ETA to the terminal only.
- <<CONFIRM>> exact current lead times if they have shifted.

## Stock & restocks
- Stock changes frequently; sold-out models are usually restocked within about a week to
  a few weeks, but there is NO guaranteed ETA.
- Customers can add their email to a product listing to be notified when it's back.

## Orders, payments & pickup
- Declined/flagged payments are usually a billing/shipping address mismatch or a card
  detail. Advise: make billing match what's on file with the card, ensure shipping
  matches billing, try a different browser, or use the PayPal checkout (no PayPal account
  needed). Luna does not control Affirm/BNPL refunds — those release automatically in a
  few business days.
- Local pickup is available at the El Segundo, CA shop. Photo ID must match the order
  name. Bike can be fully assembled at pickup for $75.
- Custom parts / price-difference upgrades: send the customer to
  https://lunacycle.com/custom-parts-order/ (set quantity = dollar amount), pay any
  shipping/labor at https://lunacycle.com/for-shipping-and-labor-payments/, then have
  them email the order number.

## Returns & refunds  (ESCALATE fee quotes to a human)
- Delivered bikes are generally NOT returnable; eligible returns incur fees and the
  customer covers freight, so refunds are partial.
- A bike still in transit can sometimes be refused with FedEx and reconsigned back.
- <<CONFIRM>> the exact reconsignment/restock fee amount before quoting it. Do NOT invent
  a dollar figure. If unknown, say you'll confirm the exact fee.

## Model-specific facts
- MX5 display works ONLY on the MX5. MX4/MX5 parts are generally NOT cross-compatible.
- The MX5 battery/cover is larger/taller and will not fit an MX3/MX4 shell. Going to 72V
  on an MX3/MX4 needs a different controller, not just a battery.
- Luna X2/X2.5, Eclipse are Luna e-bikes (mid-drive motor). Luna Fixed is belt/gearbox
  driven; its motor comes as a complete gearbox unit (removes from the left side).
- Frame bolts for X2/X2.5/Z1 (Dengfu frames): the Dengfu E22 bolt is the correct match
  (https://www.dengfubike.com/products/bolt).

## Diagnostics (technical tickets)
- Battery won't charge: have them press the bike's power button first, then connect the
  charger (newer bikes need the bike powered on); try charging the pack out of the frame.
- A flashing battery-gauge percentage is an error code — ask them to count the fast
  flashes before the ~2-second pause (there can be more than one code).
- For error codes generally, first ask whether the bike still runs when the throttle is
  twisted; ask for the exact code and a short video.
- Persistent no-charge after a charge-port replacement usually means a BMS fault.
- Video uploads over 20MB: have them use a free streamable.com account and send the link.

## Warranty  (ESCALATE approval to a human)
- Warranty covers manufacturing defects for the ORIGINAL owner only; it does NOT transfer
  if the bike was bought used/second-hand.
- To process a warranty part, request: a photo of the VIN (stamped on the RIGHT side of
  the head tube next to the manufacturer plate; starts with R73 or CTL) and a photo of
  the relevant component's serial number.
- <<CONFIRM/INSERT>> current warranty length/terms.

## Speed / derestrict  (POLICY — do not instruct)
- All Talaria bikes ship locked to 20 mph out of the box for legal reasons.
- For liability reasons, Luna does NOT provide derestrict/unlock instructions. Do not
  describe how (e.g., which wire to cut). It is acceptable to note it's well documented on
  third-party YouTube channels (e.g., Talaria Boys) and that these are off-road bikes.

## Always escalate to a human (draft should recommend, not resolve)
- Refund amounts and fee quotes; fraud/chargeback decisions.
- Battery safety incidents (smoke, heat, swelling) — advise moving the battery somewhere
  safe away from heat/flammables and flag for urgent human follow-up.
- Legal threats, injury claims, or anything requiring a judgment call.
